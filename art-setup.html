Accepted file types

We accept artwork as a print ready PDF. A correctly exported PDF locks in your fonts, images and colours so what you approve is what gets printed.

Recommended programs for setting up print artwork:

  • Adobe InDesign or Illustrator - the industry standard for print.
  • Affinity and other professional vector design programs.
  • Canva - fine for simple jobs without advanced finishing. Export as "PDF Print" with crop marks and bleed ticked.

Office programs like Word and PowerPoint, and AI image generators, cannot natively produce print ready artwork. If that's all you have, send it through and we'll quote you to rebuild it properly.

Bleed and trim marks

Bleed is printing that extends beyond the trim edge. Commercial guillotines cut through thick stacks of stock, and a tiny amount of movement is normal. Bleed makes sure that movement never leaves a white sliver on your edge.

We require 3mm bleed on every edge. An A4 (210 × 297mm) file with bleed becomes 216 × 303mm. Any colour or image touching the edge of your design must extend into that bleed zone.

Most design programs let you set bleed on the document at setup and include it, with crop marks, when you export your PDF.

Flatten your file

Please flatten your PDF before sending it through. Layered files with live transparency, effects or embedded objects can behave unpredictably when the press software pulls them apart. A flattened file prints exactly the way it looks.

Colour: CMYK, not RGB

Screens display colour with light (RGB). Presses print colour with ink (CMYK). All files must be supplied in CMYK - if you supply RGB, the conversion can shift your colours away from what you saw on screen. Check your proof carefully with that in mind.

If exact colour matters, give us PMS colour codes, a CMYK breakdown, or a previously printed sample we can match to.

Rich black: for solid black backgrounds and fills, use C:60 M:40 Y:40 K:100. Plain 100% K black on large areas can print grey and mottled. Keep small text at 100% K only.

Resolution

Print needs a minimum of 300dpi at the final size. Text heavy artwork is sharpest at 600dpi. Images saved from the web are usually 72dpi - they'll look fine on your screen and fuzzy on paper. If we flag your artwork as low resolution on the proof, take it seriously.

Copyright and licensing

You must own or be licensed to use every image, logo and font in your artwork. We accept no responsibility for the copyright status of files supplied for printing - see our Terms of Trade.

Proofing and preflight

Every job is preflight checked before production. We'll flag missing bleed, low resolution, RGB colour and other issues, and you'll receive a proof to approve before anything is printed. Production starts once your proof is approved and payment is received - so the proof stage is your moment to check everything: spelling, numbers, dates, layout and colour.
